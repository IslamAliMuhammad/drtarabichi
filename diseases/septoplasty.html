SEPTOPLASTY

About:-

A deviated nasal septum is a condition in which the wall dividing the two nostrils (the septum) is displaced or bent to one side. This can occur because of injury or congenital defects, and can cause a range of symptoms, including nasal congestion, difficulty breathing through one or both nostrils, frequent nosebleeds, and facial pain.

Indications-

Septoplasty is a surgical procedure to correct a deviated nasal septum. The following are the common indications for a septoplasty:

1. Nasal obstruction or blockage: Difficulty breathing through the nose due to a deviated septum.

2. Recurrent sinus infections: A deviated septum can make it difficult to drain the sinuses and increase the risk of sinus infections.

3. Chronic nasal congestion: Persistent nasal congestion due to a deviated septum can cause difficulty breathing and sleep problems.

4. Nosebleeds: A deviated septum can dry out the nasal passages and lead to frequent nosebleeds.

5. Facial pain: A deviated septum can cause pain or pressure in the face, especially near the affected nostril.

6. Sleep apnea: A deviated septum can contribute to sleep apnea, a condition in which a person stops breathing during sleep.

It is important to note that septoplasty may not be necessary for everyone with a deviated septum, as some people may have mild symptoms that can be managed with other treatments. The decision to undergo septoplasty should be made after careful evaluation and consultation with a healthcare provider.

RECOVERY: -

Here are some general guidelines for recovering from septoplasty:

1. Rest: Following the surgery, it is important to rest and avoid strenuous activities, such as heavy lifting or exercise, to allow the body to heal.

2. Cold compresses: Applying cold compresses to the nose can help to reduce swelling and pain.

3. Pain management: Pain medication can be used to manage discomfort following the surgery.

4. Nasal irrigation: Irrigating the nose with a saline solution can help to keep the nasal passages clean and reduce the risk of infection.

5. Avoid blowing your nose: Avoid blowing your nose for the first few days after the surgery, as this can put pressure on the healing septum and increase the risk of bleeding.

6. Follow the surgeon's instructions: It is important to follow all of the surgeon's instructions for post-operative care, including when to return for follow-up visits and when to start resuming normal activities.

7. Avoid alcohol and smoking: Alcohol and smoking can interfere with the healing process and increase the risk of complications following the surgery, so it is important to avoid these substances.

It is important to keep in mind that recovery from septoplasty can take several weeks, and everyone's experience will be different. If you have concerns or questions about your recovery, it is important to talk to your surgeon.
