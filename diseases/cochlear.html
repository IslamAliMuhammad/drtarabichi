Cochlear Implant Surgery

What is a cochlear implant?

A cochlear implant is a small electronic device that is surgically implanted in the ear to help provide a sense of sound to people who are deaf or severely hard of hearing. It consists of an external processor that sits behind the ear and an internal device that is implanted in the ear.

Who is a candidate for a cochlear implant?

Cochlear implants are typically recommended for individuals who have a severe or profound hearing loss and are not able to benefit from hearing aids. They may also be recommended for individuals who have a severe hearing loss in one ear and normal hearing in the other ear.

How does a cochlear implant work?

The external processor of a cochlear implant converts sounds into electrical signals, which are then transmitted to the internal device through a small wire. The internal device is implanted in the cochlea, the part of the inner ear responsible for hearing. The electrical signals are then converted into sound and sent to the brain, allowing the person to hear.

Is a cochlear implant a cure for deafness?

A cochlear implant can provide a sense of sound to individuals who are deaf or severely hard of hearing, but it is not a cure for deafness. The brain still needs to learn how to interpret the sounds that the cochlear implant provides, and this process can take time. Some people may still have difficulty understanding speech, even with a cochlear implant.

How long does a cochlear implant last?

Cochlear implants are designed to last for many years, but the external components may need to be replaced every 5-10 years due to wear and tear. The internal components typically last longer, but may need to be replaced at some point.

Are there any risks associated with cochlear implants?

As with any surgical procedure, there are risks associated with cochlear implants. These may include infection, bleeding, or damage to the inner ear. However, the risks are generally low and most people who receive a cochlear implant experience significant improvement in their ability to hear.
