Adenoidectomy

1. What are adenoids?

Adenoid is a patch of lymphatic tissue occupying the airway space behind the nose. Adenoids helps to fight against inhaled pathogens in childhood. It remains enlarged during early childhood, then starts shrinking in size and disappears by puberty.

2. When do you have to surgically remove it?

In few, adenoid gland keeps enlarging and blocks the air passage behind the nose and also blocks the eustachian tube. On blocking the Eustachian tube, it eventually prevents the aeration and drainage of the middle ear resulting in a negative pressure and fluid retention in ear.

3. How you know that you have enlarged adenoids?

Your child will be breathing through the mouth, will have a nasal tone on talking (talking with a stuffed nose), repeated middle ear infection and Snoring.

4. What is adenoid facies?

Your child’s face will show certain changes in shape due to the presence of a long standing enlarged adenoid gland. The child will have a wide intercanthal distance, pinched nose, high arched palate, crowded teeth, persistently opened mouth, and short upper lip are few of them.

5. Will the adenoid facies gets corrected/reversed after the surgery of the adenoid gland?

No, the adenoid facies do not get reversed with the adenoidectomy surgery. They need complex bone reshaping surgical procedures to correct it later.

6. What other issues can happen to a child with adenoid gland enlarged?

Child can develop hearing loss and recurrent ear infections, persistent nasal and sinus infection, breathing issues while sleeping (sleep-apnoea syndrome) and even heart issues(cor-pulmonale) if left untreated.
