Tonsillectomy

1. How is a tonsillectomy performed?

A tonsillectomy is typically performed under general anesthesia, which means you will be asleep during the procedure. The surgeon will remove the tonsils using a scalpel or a laser. The procedure typically takes about 30-45 minutes.

Recovery from a tonsillectomy typically takes about a week. During this time, you may experience pain and swelling in your throat, as well as difficulty swallowing. Your healthcare provider will prescribe pain medication to help manage any discomfort. It is important to follow the provider's instructions for caring for the surgical site and taking any prescribed medications.

3. Are there any risks or complications associated with a tonsillectomy?

As with any surgery, there are risks and complications associated with a tonsillectomy. These may include bleeding, infection, and allergic reactions to the anesthesia. Serious complications, such as bleeding or infection, are rare. Your healthcare provider will discuss the potential risks and benefits of the procedure with you before you decide to undergo a tonsillectomy.
