What Is Meant by A Deviated Septum?

Contact Us

The septum is located in the center between the two nostrils and divides them evenly. However, in some cases, there is some change in the shape and position, and this is called a deviated septum. A common condition, an uneven septum, is not dangerous until it causes additional concerns, such as breathing difficulties.

Every organ in the ENT system plays a specific role and impacts the body’s overall harmonious functioning. The septum, for instance, is composed of both the cartilage and bone in the nose that separates both nostrils. Although essentially present to provide a structure to the nose, an uneven septum can lead to other issues. This blog discusses what a deviated septum is, the symptoms and causes of this condition and the possible treatments. Read on to know more.

What Are the Symptoms and Possible Causes of a Deviated Septum?

If you have a severe deviated nasal septum, you might have issues such as difficulty in breathing normally, sinus infections, nasal congestion, snoring or nosebleeds. Additionally, since the septum is part of the facial structural system, it can also cause facial pain. A deviated septum generally appears as a congenital condition that is present right from the time of birth. However, for one that develops sometime during the lifespan of the individual, it can commonly be associated with a nose or facial injury or trauma. Either way, a deviated septum needs medical attention if it starts affecting your ability to carry out daily activities and hence, the quality of your life.

What Are the Best Treatments for A Deviated Septum?

An ENT specialist can easily diagnose a deviated septum health condition. Surgery is considered ideal for relief if you suffer from severe consequences because of the condition. In minor cases, on the contrary, medications and treatments like decongestants and nasal strips are suggested for relief. A deviated septum is a common condition worldwide.
